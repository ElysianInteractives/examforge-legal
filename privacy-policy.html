Privacy Policy for ExamForge

Effective date: May 17, 2026

ExamForge is published by North Star Labs. This Privacy Policy explains how ExamForge handles information when you use the app.

Summary

ExamForge is designed as an offline-first study tool. Your classes, decks, flashcards, exam plans, notes, recordings, review history, settings, and backups are stored on your device unless you choose to export or share them yourself.

ExamForge does not run ads, does not use third-party advertising SDKs, and does not sell personal information.

Information You Provide or Create

ExamForge may store the following information locally on your device:

  • Class names, deck names, flashcards, tags, hints, notes, and exam details you create in the app.
  • Study progress, review history, weak-card status, quiz progress, Pomodoro activity, and reminder preferences.
  • File references for notes or recordings you choose to attach to a class.
  • Backup files you choose to create or restore.
  • Your Google account email address after Google Sign-In, used to show account status inside the app.

This information is stored locally on your device using Android app storage. ExamForge does not operate a backend server that receives or stores your study content.

Google Sign-In

ExamForge uses Google Sign-In through Android Credential Manager to let you open your study workspace with a Google account. The app may receive your Google account email address for account display and local sign-in state.

Google's handling of account information is governed by Google's own privacy policies.

Purchases and Pro Unlock

ExamForge uses Google Play Billing for Pro purchases. Purchase processing is handled by Google Play. ExamForge receives purchase status information from Google Play so it can unlock Pro features in the app.

ExamForge does not receive or store your full payment card details.

ExamForge may also support a private local unlock code for friends and family. A valid code unlocks Pro features locally on the device.

Notes, Recordings, and Backups

If you attach notes or recordings, ExamForge stores references to the files you selected so the app can help organize your class materials. The files remain under Android's file access system unless you import, export, delete, or share them using your device.

If you create a backup, the backup file may contain your classes, decks, cards, materials metadata, review state, and settings. You control where that backup file is saved and how it is shared.

Notifications

If you enable daily study reminders or use the Pomodoro timer, ExamForge may show local notifications. Notification permission is requested on Android versions that require it. These notifications are generated on your device.

Information Shared With Third Parties

ExamForge may interact with the following third-party services as part of app functionality:

  • Google Sign-In / Credential Manager: used for account sign-in.
  • Google Play Billing: used for Pro purchases and purchase restoration.
  • Google Play services / Android system services: used as part of normal Android app operation.

ExamForge does not share your study content with these services for advertising or analytics.

Data Safety

ExamForge does not intentionally collect study content to a developer-controlled server. Most user data remains on the device. Some account and purchase information may be processed by Google services when you use Google Sign-In or Google Play Billing.

Children's Privacy

ExamForge is a general study tool and is not directed specifically to children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information in a way that requires action, contact us using the email below.

Data Deletion

You can delete classes, decks, cards, materials, exams, backups, and app data from within the app or through Android system settings. Uninstalling the app removes locally stored app data from the device, subject to Android's normal backup and storage behavior.

Google account and purchase records managed by Google are controlled through your Google account and Google Play settings.

Security

ExamForge relies on Android app storage, Android file permissions, Google Sign-In, and Google Play Billing to protect app data and purchases. No method of storage or transmission is perfect, but the app is designed to minimize data sent off-device.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as ExamForge changes. The effective date above will be updated when material changes are made.
